# Backlink - Local Press Pitches

**Category:** Off-Page SEO
**Automation Readiness Score:** 4/10 - Low automation, mostly human
**Status:** SOP documented

---

## Purpose

Process 18 covers local press pitch opportunities. The goal is to earn legitimate local mentions, citations, or backlinks by pitching useful, timely, locally relevant story angles to local journalists, editors, community publishers, or niche green-industry media.

This process is not the same as Process 12, Press Releases. Press releases are prepared and uploaded through Signal GeNeSYS/Media Room workflows. Local press pitches are targeted outreach to real media contacts around story angles that might earn coverage.

The goal is simple:

- Identify pitchable local story angles tied to real client work, seasons, trends, community activity, or expertise.
- Build a small, relevant media list.
- Draft concise, factual pitches.
- Get human/client approval before outreach.
- Track sent, replied, rejected, interested, published, and blocked statuses.
- Verify live coverage, mentions, and backlinks.
- Avoid spammy outreach, fake news angles, or overstated claims.

## Target State

Every local press pitch campaign should be small, specific, and trackable.

The ideal system:

1. Starts with a real client story, expert angle, seasonal issue, community connection, or timely local topic.
2. Confirms the angle is truthful, relevant, and safe to pitch.
3. Identifies local outlets and contacts that actually cover the topic.
4. Drafts a concise pitch with useful context and a clear reason the story matters now.
5. Gets human/client approval before outreach.
6. Sends outreach through the right owner.
7. Tracks replies, follow-ups, coverage, and blockers.
8. Verifies live coverage, brand mentions, citations, and backlinks.

## Pitchable Angle Types

Prioritize useful stories that a local editor might realistically care about.

| Angle Type | Examples | Decision |
|---|---|---|
| Seasonal advice | Spring lawn prep, drought watering, storm cleanup, winter property prep | Good fit when timely and locally useful. |
| Local trend | Rising demand for outdoor living, native plants, drainage fixes, tick prevention | Good fit with evidence and service relevance. |
| Community involvement | Sponsorship, charity work, local event support | Use only when real and approved. |
| Business milestone | Anniversary, expansion, hiring, new service, award | Use when meaningful and not thin. |
| Expert commentary | Local weather, landscaping safety, seasonal maintenance tips | Good fit if client can credibly comment. |
| Project story | Notable local project or transformation | Use only with client/customer approval and photos if needed. |
| Press-release extension | Strong PR angle pitched to selected local contacts | Use when it has local news value beyond upload. |
| Weak SEO-only pitch | Generic "please link to us" ask | Reject. |

## Workflow

### 1. Confirm Scope

Start from `3. Backlink Acquisition`.

Confirm:

- The request is local media outreach, not PR upload.
- The client has a real angle worth pitching.
- The pitch target market matches the client's service area.
- Human/client approval is required before outreach.
- Any quote, claim, image, customer story, or community involvement is approved.

Do not pitch fake news, invented milestones, exaggerated expertise, or unapproved customer/project details.

### 2. Build Story Angle Options

Generate a small list of possible angles from:

- Current season.
- Local weather or property concerns.
- Client services.
- Client projects or milestones.
- Community events or sponsorships.
- Press release topics.
- Blog topics with local relevance.
- Competitor media placements.

For each angle, capture:

- Story angle.
- Why it matters locally.
- Target audience.
- Supporting facts.
- Client quote/source needed.
- Supporting URL or asset.
- Risk/sensitivity.
- Recommended outlets.
- Initial decision: approve for review, needs facts, reject, or blocked.

### 3. Validate Newsworthiness

Before drafting outreach, check:

- Is this actually useful or newsworthy?
- Is it timely?
- Is it local?
- Can the client credibly speak on it?
- Are facts and claims approved?
- Would a local editor understand why readers care?
- Is there a stronger blog/PR/service-page support URL?

Reject weak angles that are only backlink requests.

### 4. Build the Media List

Find relevant local targets:

- Local newspapers.
- Community news sites.
- Local magazines.
- Radio/TV web desks where appropriate.
- Neighborhood publications.
- Green-industry local blogs.
- City/community event calendars when relevant.
- Reporters who cover business, home, weather, environment, community, or seasonal service topics.

For each contact/outlet, capture:

- Outlet name.
- Website.
- Contact name.
- Email or form URL.
- Beat/topic fit.
- Example relevant article.
- Geographic fit.
- Pitch angle fit.
- Submission rules.
- Notes.

Do not scrape or blast irrelevant contacts. Keep the list small and relevant.

### 5. Draft the Pitch

Create:

- Subject line options.
- 2 to 4 sentence pitch body.
- Why now.
- Why this audience.
- Client/source credibility.
- Optional quote.
- Supporting URL.
- Suggested headline or story framing.
- Contact/signature.

A good pitch should be short, useful, and specific. It should not sound like an SEO link request.

Avoid:

- "Please link to us."
- Generic mass outreach.
- Overstated claims.
- Exact-match anchor text requests.
- Attachments unless requested.
- Customer/project details without approval.

### 6. Human/Client Approval

Human approval is required before:

- Sending any pitch.
- Using a client quote.
- Naming a customer, employee, partner, or organization.
- Sharing photos or project details.
- Making claims about awards, certifications, demand, safety, weather, or pricing.
- Following up with a journalist.

Approval should define:

- Final angle.
- Approved facts.
- Approved quote/source.
- Supporting URL.
- Sender.
- Target media list.
- Follow-up cadence.

### 7. Send or Assign Outreach

Humans manage outreach when:

- Reporter relationship matters.
- Pitch uses a client spokesperson.
- Outlet requires personal contact.
- Client approval is conditional.
- The topic is sensitive.

Koga can prepare final copy, contact list, and tracking rows. Do not send external emails or forms unless explicitly authorized for that exact campaign.

### 8. Track Responses and Follow-Ups

Track:

- Date sent.
- Sender.
- Outlet/contact.
- Pitch angle.
- Status.
- Reply summary.
- Follow-up date.
- Coverage URL.
- Link/mention status.
- Notes.

Use statuses consistently:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Candidate | Angle or outlet found, not approved. |
| Approved | Ready for outreach. |
| Sent | Pitch sent. |
| Follow-up scheduled | Follow-up date set. |
| Interested | Reporter/editor replied positively. |
| Declined | Outlet passed. |
| Published | Coverage is live. |
| Mention only | Coverage exists but no link. |
| Blocked | Waiting on approval, facts, contact, or asset. |

### 9. Verify Coverage and Backlink

When coverage appears, verify:

- Coverage URL is live.
- Client name is correct.
- Website link exists if included.
- Link points to the correct URL.
- Mention does not include wrong NAP, service areas, claims, or quotes.
- Article/page is publicly accessible.
- Any images/logos are approved.
- Backlink tracking file is updated.

If coverage mentions the client without a link, track it as a brand/local mention, not a backlink win.

## Completion Criteria

The process is complete when:

- Pitch angles are researched and approved or rejected.
- Media targets are relevant and documented.
- Approved pitches are prepared or sent by the correct owner.
- Follow-ups are scheduled where appropriate.
- Published coverage is verified.
- Links and mentions are classified accurately.
- Backlink tracking file is updated.
- ClickUp reflects the true status.

## Common Mistakes

- Treating local press pitches like press-release uploads.
- Sending generic backlink requests to journalists.
- Pitching fake or weak story angles.
- Using unapproved customer/project details.
- Asking for exact-match anchor text.
- Contacting irrelevant reporters.
- Marking a mention as a backlink when there is no link.
- Forgetting to track follow-ups and replies.
- Using the Signal GeNeSYS upload video as if it teaches media pitching.
