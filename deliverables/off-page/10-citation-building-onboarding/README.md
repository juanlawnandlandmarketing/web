# Citation Building — Onboarding

**Category:** Off-Page SEO
**Automation Readiness Score:** 4/10 — 🟡 Low automation — mostly human
**Status:** ✅ Documented

---

## Purpose

Citation building creates consistent business mentions across important local directories, data platforms, industry sites, and profile sources. For local SEO, the work matters because search engines need to see the same business identity repeated across trusted places: name, address, phone, website, categories, hours, services, and service area details.

For Lawn & Land clients, citation work is not "submit to every directory on the internet." The goal is to build accurate core listings, avoid NAP confusion, remove obvious inconsistencies, and hand the account into ongoing monitoring with a clean source of truth.

The goal is simple:

- Confirm one approved NAP source of truth before any submissions.
- Build or correct the highest-value citation profiles during onboarding.
- Keep business identity consistent with the website and GBP.
- Avoid duplicate, outdated, fake, or low-quality listings.
- Document every submission, login dependency, verification blocker, and live URL.
- Hand off unresolved issues to Process 11, Citation Monitoring.

## Current State

Citation building appears in the fulfillment map as a one-time onboarding task and connects to several related workflows:

- ClickUp task `12. Citation Building` is the primary task source for this onboarding SOP.
- ClickUp subtask `White spark order submit (Pending for a launch)` is the core Whitespark submission task.
- ClickUp task `5. Clear NAP Listings` and subtask `Verify NAP in Whitespark for SAE Clients` define the NAP verification path that supports citation onboarding.
- ClickUp task `1. Business Directory Management (Whitespark)` is the ongoing Whitespark management workflow that citation onboarding hands into after launch.
- GBP profile setup needs accurate business name, phone, website, hours, services, and service area details.
- Service-area work depends on truthful service coverage.
- Competitor analysis may reveal directory pressure or authority gaps.
- Business-directory backlinks may overlap with citation opportunities.
- Ongoing citation monitoring needs a clean baseline to compare against.

The missing piece is a standalone citation onboarding process that turns those ClickUp tasks into one clear SOP: business data packet, Whitespark order preparation, directory priority, submission rules, documentation format, video/training references, and human approval gates.

## Target State

Every new SEO client should have a citation onboarding packet and completion log.

The ideal system:

1. Collects approved business identity data.
2. Confirms the website, GBP, and client-provided records agree.
3. Builds a prioritized directory list.
4. Flags duplicates, old addresses, old phone numbers, wrong domains, and wrong categories.
5. Prepares submission-ready business descriptions, categories, services, photos, and links.
6. Separates safe preparation work from human/manual submission steps.
7. Tracks live listing URLs, pending verifications, blocked listings, and follow-up needs.
8. Hands the final citation baseline into ongoing monitoring.

## Automation Score

**4/10 — Low automation, mostly human**

Koga can automate meaningful prep and QA:

- Check the website and GBP for consistency.
- Create a directory priority list.

## Citation Source Types

Prioritize citation sources by trust, relevance, and maintenance value.

| Source Type | Examples | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| Core local data platforms | Major map/data aggregators and high-trust local directories | High |
| Search and map profiles | Google, Bing, Apple, other map surfaces when applicable | High |
| General business directories | Yelp, BBB, Chamber pages, local business listings | Medium to high depending on market |
| Green-industry directories | Lawn, landscape, hardscape, nursery, irrigation, or contractor directories | Medium |
| Local community sites | City, county, chamber, neighborhood, event, or sponsorship pages | Medium |
| Social/entity profiles | Facebook, LinkedIn, Instagram, YouTube, Nextdoor where applicable | Medium |
| Low-quality directories | Thin scraper sites, spam directories, paid-only junk | Reject unless there is a specific reason |

Citation building and backlink building overlap, but they are not identical. Citation onboarding focuses on business identity consistency. Backlink campaigns focus on authority and relationship opportunities.

## Workflow

### 1. Lock the NAP Source of Truth

Do not submit citations until the business identity is approved.

Confirm:

- Legal or public-facing business name.
- Whether the business uses a DBA.
- Street address or service-area business status.
- Phone number to publish.
- Website URL to use.
- Hours.
- Primary categories.
- Service list.
- Service area.
- GBP profile URL.
- Social URLs.

Use `Verify NAP in website and GBP` and `Verify NAP in Whitespark for SAE Clients` as the operational checks for this step. If the website, GBP, client notes, Whitespark, and existing listings disagree, stop and resolve the conflict before submitting new citations.

### 2. Build the Citation Packet

Prepare one reusable packet for submissions.

Include:

- Business name.
- Address.
- Phone.
- Website.
- Email to use when allowed.
- Categories.
- Short description.
- Long description.
- Services.
- Service areas.
- Hours.
- Logo.
- Photos.
- Social URLs.
- Owner/contact name when required.
- Notes on service-area business behavior.
- Do-not-use fields, such as old phone numbers, old URLs, or old addresses.

Descriptions should be plain, factual, and consistent. Do not invent awards, licenses, emergency service, guarantees, service areas, or years in business.

### 3. Find Existing Listings and Duplicates

Before creating new listings, search for:

- Existing correct listings.
- Listings with old phone numbers.
- Listings with old addresses.
- Listings with old domains.
- Duplicate profiles.
- Listings claimed by old vendors.
- Incorrect categories.
- Closed or moved status.
- Profiles for previous business names.

When a listing already exists, prefer correcting or claiming the existing profile instead of creating another duplicate.

### 4. Prioritize the Submission List

Build a directory list in tiers.

| Tier | Rule |
|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Must-have sources that affect entity trust, maps, or major local discovery. |
| Tier 2 | Strong local, industry, chamber, or review sources that fit the client. |
| Tier 3 | Useful but lower-priority directories or niche opportunities. |
| Reject | Spam, irrelevant, duplicate, risky, or low-quality platforms. |

For each platform, capture:

- Platform name.
- URL.
- Tier.
- Listing status.
- Existing listing URL if found.
- Account required.
- Verification required.
- Payment required.
- Login owner.
- Submission owner.
- Notes.

### 5. Prepare Submission Copy

For each approved platform, prepare:

- Correct NAP.
- Category choice.
- Description variant if needed.
- Service list.
- Service area.
- Website URL.
- Photos/logo where allowed.
- Social links.
- Notes for fields that must be skipped or handled manually.

Keep descriptions consistent, but avoid pasting identical keyword-stuffed text everywhere. The business identity should be stable; profile copy can be lightly adapted to the platform.

### 6. Submit or Assign Manual Steps

For Whitespark orders, use the `White spark order submit (Pending for a launch)` task as the submission path. Treat the order as launch-ready only after the NAP source of truth is locked, the citation packet is complete, and any payment, access, or verification blocker has a human owner.

Classify each listing before action:

| Submission Type | Rule |
|---|---|
| Safe prep only | Koga prepares the packet, but a human submits because account access or verification is required. |
| Human submission | Human creates/claims/edits the listing directly. |
| Assisted submission | Koga drafts fields and the human performs the final platform action. |
| Already correct | Log the listing URL and no action needed. |
| Needs cleanup | Duplicate, wrong NAP, old address, or old vendor claim requires manual resolution. |
| Blocked | Needs client verification, payment approval, phone/email code, or account access. |

Do not use Matt-owned or team-owned credentials to create third-party accounts for someone else unless the right authorization exists. Do not submit paid listings without explicit approval.

### 7. Verify and Log Results

After submission or correction, log:

- Platform.
- Listing URL.
- Submitted/corrected fields.
- Status: live, pending, blocked, rejected, duplicate, already correct.
- Verification method.
- Owner.
- Date submitted.
- Date verified.
- Remaining follow-up.

Verify live listings when possible. If the listing is pending moderation, log the pending status and follow-up date instead of calling it complete.

### 8. Handoff to Citation Monitoring

At the end of onboarding, hand off:

- Approved NAP packet.
- Completed listing URLs.
- Pending verification items.
- Duplicate suppression items.
- Blocked platforms.
- Old NAP variants discovered.
- Next check date.

Process 11 should use this baseline for ongoing citation monitoring.

## Completion Criteria

The process is complete when:

- The approved NAP source of truth is documented.
- The citation packet is complete.
- Existing listings and duplicates have been checked on priority platforms.
- Tier 1 citation sources are completed, already correct, or clearly blocked.
- Tier 2 and Tier 3 opportunities are submitted, assigned, rejected, or scheduled.
- Live listing URLs are logged when available.
- Pending verification and duplicate cleanup items have owners and follow-up dates.
- The final citation baseline is ready for ongoing monitoring.

## Common Mistakes

- Submitting before the NAP source of truth is approved.
- Creating duplicate listings instead of claiming or correcting existing ones.
- Using different phone numbers across platforms without a tracking decision.
- Listing a service-area business like it has a staffed public office when it does not.
- Adding services, categories, awards, licenses, or service areas that are not real.
- Paying for low-quality directories without approval.
- Calling pending listings complete.
- Forgetting to document login, verification, or ownership blockers.
- Skipping the handoff to citation monitoring.
