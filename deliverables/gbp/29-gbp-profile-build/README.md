# GBP Profile Build & Completion

**Category:** GBP SEO
**Automation Readiness Score:** 2/10 - Manual-heavy
**Status:** SOP documented

---

## Purpose

Process 29 builds or audits a Google Business Profile so the listing is accurate, complete, compliant, and ready for ongoing GBP SEO work. The goal is not to stuff the profile with every possible field. The goal is to match the real business, avoid verification or suspension risk, and complete the fields that help customers and Google understand the company.

This process covers:

- Business name accuracy.
- Location/address visibility rules.
- Service-area setup.
- Primary and secondary category setup.
- Phone numbers.
- Website URL.
- Business hours and special hours.
- Description/bio.
- Services.
- Products when appropriate.
- Photos, logo, and cover photo.
- Social profile links.
- Q&A seed content when approved.
- Review and NAP consistency handoff.
- Verification and re-verification risk controls.

## Target State

Every GBP profile build or completion audit should produce:

1. Approved source-of-truth packet for business name, NAP, website, services, service areas, hours, and category.
2. GBP access/verification status check.
3. Field-by-field completion checklist.
4. Risk log for changes that may trigger verification or policy issues.
5. Human-approved edits for sensitive profile fields.
6. Drafted bio, services, products, Q&A, and photo/file recommendations.
7. Screenshots or notes proving each completed field.
8. Handoffs to category, posts, reviews, photos, Q&A, website link, and attributes processes.
9. Final ClickUp update with what was completed, what was blocked, and what needs client/Matt/Juan approval.

## Compliance Rules to Respect

Do not make GBP edits that conflict with Google guidelines or approved client facts.

Important rules:

- The business name must reflect the real-world name, not keyword stuffing.
- The profile must represent a real eligible business.
- Address visibility must match how the business serves customers.
- Service-area businesses should not expose a residential/private address just to complete the field.
- Categories must describe what the business is, not every service it sells.
- Hours must be accurate.
- Phone and website should route to the real business/approved tracking setup.
- Description should describe the business and services truthfully without promotional gimmicks or prohibited content.
- Photos should represent the business, team, work, equipment, logo, or real service context.
- Do not change sensitive fields on an existing GBP unless the risk is understood and approved.
- If an edit triggers verification or re-verification, stop and document the blocker.

## Sensitive Fields

These fields require human approval before changing:

| Field | Why It Is Sensitive |
|---|---|
| Business name | Keyword stuffing or mismatch can create suspension risk. |
| Address / address visibility | Can trigger verification and must reflect business model. |
| Primary category | Major ranking and eligibility signal. |
| Phone number | Affects calls, tracking, verification, and NAP consistency. |
| Website URL | Affects conversions, tracking, and profile trust. |
| Hours | Customer-facing and can affect trust. |
| Service areas | Can misrepresent coverage if not client-approved. |
| Verification method/status | Requires account owner action and often client/Matt/Juan coordination. |
| Ownership/manager access | Security write; do not add/remove users without Matt approval. |

## Workflow

### 1. Confirm Scope and Access

Start with `10. Pre Launch Website GBP Optimization`.

Record:

- Client name.
- GBP status: new, existing, claimed, unclaimed, suspended, duplicate, pending verification, verified.
- Who owns access.
- Whether this is pre-launch build, post-launch completion, or quarterly completion audit.
- Whether edits are approved or audit-only.
- Risky fields that should not be changed without approval.

If profile ownership, user access, or manager invites are needed, stop. That is a security/access action and requires Matt's explicit approval.

### 2. Lock the Source of Truth

Gather approved client facts from:

- Client Directory Info.
- Ground Control/client source-of-truth records.
- Website contact/footer pages.
- GBP existing profile.
- Client onboarding docs.
- Approved phone/tracking number records.
- Approved service and service-area list.
- Approved logo/photo folder.

Do not invent missing facts. Mark them as blockers.

Source-of-truth packet:

| Field | Required Evidence |
|---|---|
| Business name | Exact legal/brand display name from approved source. |
| Primary phone | Approved marketing/tracking/client phone rule. |
| Additional phone | Client-provided connect number when applicable. |
| Website | Approved homepage or campaign URL. |
| Address | Approved address/private-address rule. |
| Service areas | Approved city list, prioritized top cities. |
| Hours | Approved operating hours and special hours if known. |
| Services | Approved sold services. |
| Photos/logo | Approved media assets. |

### 3. Accurate Name

Use ClickUp `Accurate Name`.

Rules:

- Copy the approved business name exactly.
- Do not add city names, service keywords, taglines, phone numbers, or punctuation not used by the business.
- Match capitalization and punctuation from the approved source.
- Compare against website, GBP, client docs, and citation source.

If the existing profile name differs from the approved name, document the risk and get human approval before changing it.

### 4. Category Setup

Use ClickUp `Primary Category` and `Secondary Categories`.

For Process 29, confirm that categories are present and plausible. Deeper category research belongs in Process 30.

Baseline rules:

- Primary category should match the main business type.
- Secondary categories should be directly relevant.
- Do not add loosely related categories just because they contain keywords.
- Do not add every service as a category.
- Leave secondary categories blank if no accurate category exists.

Route detailed competitor/category research to Process 30.

### 5. Location and Address

Use ClickUp `Location / Address`.

Decision rules:

| Business Type | Address Handling |
|---|---|
| Storefront/client-facing office | Address may be visible if customers visit during stated hours. |
| Service-area business / home-based contractor | Hide private/residential address and use service areas. |
| Existing GBP with stable verified address | Do not change address unless approved. |
| New GBP | Use approved address/private-address setup and document verification risk. |

ClickUp instruction says existing GBPs should make no address changes and new GBPs should be set to private with address hidden when appropriate. Follow that unless Juan/Matt provides a different approved instruction.

### 6. Phone Number

Use ClickUp `Phone Number`.

Rules:

- Primary phone should follow L&L's approved client phone/tracking setup.
- Additional phone can use the client-provided connect number when approved.
- Confirm visible phone consistency with website and NAP source.
- Confirm numbers are callable and formatted correctly.
- Do not replace phone numbers on an existing verified profile without approval.

Route broader NAP mismatches to citation monitoring / NAP cleanup.

### 7. Website URL

Use ClickUp `Website`.

Rules:

- Use the approved homepage URL unless a specific landing page is approved.
- Confirm URL loads over HTTPS.
- Confirm it is the correct client site.
- Avoid UTM/tracking changes unless approved.
- If GBP should link to a review, appointment, or service URL in another field, document separately.

After profile setup, Process 35 checks whether the website links back to the correct GBP/review URL.

### 8. Service Area

Use ClickUp `Service Area`.

Rules:

- Use approved cities, not counties, when ClickUp/client instructions require city-level targeting.
- Prioritize top 3 and top 5 markets first.
- Do not add cities the client does not serve.
- Avoid overstating the market area.
- Document if Google limits available service-area entries.

Service-area strategy should align with service-area pages, keyword map, and client service coverage.

### 9. Business Hours

Use ClickUp `Business Hours`.

Rules:

- Enter hours exactly from the approved source.
- If the client is appointment-only, 24/7, seasonal, or emergency-service based, confirm the correct display approach with a human.
- Add special hours when known.
- Do not guess holiday hours.

### 10. Description / Bio

Use ClickUp `Description / Bio`.

Draft rules from ClickUp:

- Under 750 characters.
- Written in first person plural when appropriate.
- Include primary category, core services, service areas, and years in business if approved.
- Keep it factual and natural.

Additional rules:

- No promotional hype.
- No keyword stuffing.
- No claims that are not supported by client facts.
- No phone numbers or URLs inside the description unless explicitly allowed/approved.
- No fake local claims.

Human approval is required when the description includes awards, licenses, guarantees, years in business, pricing, emergency availability, or claims not found in source materials.

### 11. Services

Use ClickUp `Add "Services"`.

Rules:

- Add approved services under relevant GBP categories.
- Use actual client services, not generic keyword variants.
- Draft concise service descriptions if the profile supports them.
- Keep names customer-friendly.
- Do not add services the client does not sell.
- Do not use services to compensate for bad categories.

Route ongoing service description audits to GBP Audit and Image Post / Process 33 or Process 36 as appropriate.

### 12. Products

Use ClickUp `Add "Products"`.

For service businesses, "Products" may be used as a visual/service merchandising area when appropriate and allowed by the profile.

Rules from ClickUp:

- Set Category = Core Service when using products for service groups.
- Product Name = individual service.
- Description = short service description.
- Leave Price and Landing URL blank unless approved.
- Use appropriate images.

Additional rules:

- Do not misrepresent services as physical products if the presentation is confusing.
- Do not use stock images if the client or profile policy/context requires real images.
- Do not add landing URLs unless the page is live and approved.
- Link Products handoff belongs to post-launch GBP optimization when URLs are ready.

### 13. Photos, Logo, and Cover Photo

Use ClickUp `Logo`, `Cover Photo`, and `Additional Images`.

Rules:

- Logo should be square, clean, and readable with enough negative space.
- Avoid logos with phone numbers, clutter, or tiny text.
- Cover photo should be high quality and client-provided when available.
- Additional images should be real, approved, and relevant.
- Avoid stock photos when client-specific photos are available or when ClickUp says client-provided only.
- Avoid images with phone numbers or promotional text.
- Recheck after upload for rejections.

Ongoing photo collection, naming, upload cadence, and freshness belongs in Process 33.

### 14. Social Profiles

Use ClickUp `Social Profile`.

Rules:

- Add only official, active, working social links.
- Verify each URL leads to the correct business.
- Do not add duplicate, abandoned, wrong-market, or personal profiles.
- Compare with website footer/header social links and citation records.

If social profiles need to be created or claimed, route to the social/backlink/entity workflow and human access owner.

### 15. Q&A Submitted

Use ClickUp `Q&As Submitted`.

Rules:

- Draft 10 helpful questions and answers only when this is part of the assigned GBP build.
- Use People Also Ask, client services, service areas, and common customer objections.
- Keep answers short, accurate, and client-approved.
- Do not publish risky claims, prices, warranties, licensing, or emergency availability without approval.
- Route ongoing Q&A monitoring and public question handling to Process 34.

### 16. Verification and Re-Verification

Before sensitive edits, check:

- Is the profile verified?
- Is verification pending?
- Is there a duplicate profile?
- Is the profile suspended?
- Is the requested edit likely to trigger re-verification?

If verification triggers:

1. Stop additional edits.
2. Screenshot/document the prompt.
3. Record the required verification method.
4. Notify the human owner.
5. Update ClickUp with blocker status.

Do not attempt verification workarounds or profile ownership changes.

### 17. Final Completeness Audit

Confirm:

- Name.
- Primary category.
- Secondary categories.
- Address/address-hidden rule.
- Service areas.
- Phone.
- Website.
- Hours.
- Description.
- Services.
- Products if used.
- Logo.
- Cover photo.
- Additional photos.
- Social profiles.
- Q&A if included.
- Review/link handoff.
- NAP consistency handoff.
- Verification status.

Mark blockers clearly. A profile with verification or approval blockers can be "audited/prepared" but not fully complete.

## Completion Criteria

Process 29 is complete only when:

- Source-of-truth packet is locked.
- GBP verification/access status is documented.
- All safe fields are completed or audited.
- Sensitive fields have human approval before edits.
- Description, services, products, photos, and Q&A are drafted/applied as assigned.
- Address/service-area/phone/website choices match approved client facts.
- Verification blockers are documented instead of bypassed.
- Handoffs are created for categories, posts, reviews, photos, Q&A, website links, and attributes.
- ClickUp reflects the real final status.

## Common Mistakes

- Keyword-stuffing the business name.
- Changing an existing profile address without approval.
- Showing a private/residential address for a service-area business.
- Adding service areas the client does not actually serve.
- Choosing categories because they have keywords instead of matching the business type.
- Adding every service as a category.
- Guessing hours, years in business, licenses, awards, or warranties.
- Using stock images when ClickUp/client instructions require client-provided images.
- Uploading logo images with tiny text or phone numbers.
- Publishing Q&A without client/account approval.
- Ignoring verification prompts.
- Treating "prepared" as complete when access or verification is blocked.
